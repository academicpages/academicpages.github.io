Overview

About

I am a Computer Science student interested in how quantitative methods, behavioural experiments, and scientific imaging can be combined to study biological systems. My work has moved between wet-lab environments, computational modelling, and tool development for research analysis.

This homepage integrates details from the current CV and provisional transcript, including coursework, research appointments, teaching activity, technical skills, and academic standing as of 13 January 2026.

At Ashoka University, I am training across computer science, biology, and psychology, with ongoing research in neuroethology and quantitative neuroscience. My recent work includes automated zebrafish movement analysis, behavioural paradigm design, and computational approaches to neural systems.

Outside Ashoka, I have worked with the Neural Systems Lab at Hyderabad Central University and attended the European Molecular Biology Laboratory Summer School in Heidelberg, where I used optical tweezers, confocal imaging, kymograph analysis, and 3D reconstruction workflows to investigate biophysical and developmental questions.

Automated Zebrafish Movement Analysis

Neuro-ethology Lab, Ashoka University

Developing an automated behavioural analysis pipeline for zebrafish experiments using computer vision, with the long-term goal of reducing manual annotation and supporting generalized movement analysis across recording setups.

Outcome: supporting closed-loop experimental design, cleaner video workflows, and numerical cognition studies in zebrafish.

  • OpenCV
  • YOLO
  • Behaviour tracking
  • Protocol design

Grid Cell Distortion Under Reward

Computational neuroscience modelling

Modelled reward-driven distortion of grid cell activity using attractor dynamics and reinforcement-based action selection, with an emphasis on how learning and navigation reshape internal representations.

Outcome: a compact modelling framework for thinking about spatial coding under behavioural reward.

  • Dynamical systems
  • Reinforcement learning
  • Neural modelling
  • Simulation

High-Performance Disk Analysis Tool

Systems software project

Built an asynchronous Rust application for file-system traversal, duplicate detection through cryptographic hashing, and disk-usage exploration, paired with a Tauri interface for real-time progress and cleanup workflows.

Outcome: a practical systems tool that combines low-level performance with a usable analytical interface.

  • Rust
  • Tauri
  • Hashing
  • Async systems

PLASH Brick Materials Project
