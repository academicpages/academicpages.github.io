# Bohan Feng (Dylan Bo) | 冯波瀚
**Lecturer, Robotics & Intelligent Manufacturing Researcher**

Hi there! I am a Lecturer at the Global Institute of Future Technology (GIFT), Shanghai Jiao Tong University. My research focuses on **complex decision-making and mechanistic modeling for robots in intelligent manufacturing** — bridging planning, learning, and optimization to enable autonomous robot behavior in dynamic, uncertain industrial environments. I received my Ph.D. in Control Science and Engineering from Shanghai Jiao Tong University (2025, advised by Prof. Yunbo Bi), and my B.Eng. in Computer Science and Technology from Dalian University of Technology (2020).

I have published **10 SCI-indexed papers and 3 EI papers**, including 6 as first author, with a cumulative journal impact factor of 90+, in venues including *Robotics and Computer-Integrated Manufacturing*, *Expert Systems with Applications*, *Advanced Science*, and *The Innovation*. Research collaborations are welcome!

## <span class="lang-zh">研究问题</span><span class="lang-en">Research Questions</span>

### <span class="lang-zh">1. 机器人如何在复杂动态环境中实现高效、可靠的运动规划？</span><span class="lang-en">1. How Can Robots Plan Efficient, Reliable Motions in Complex Dynamic Environments?</span>

<div class="lang-zh" markdown="1">
工业机器人需在杂乱、动态的工作空间中高效作业，经典规划方法面临可扩展性与适应性瓶颈。我从采样优化（自适应多树RRT）、神经架构设计（BRMPNet双向循环网络）到序列生成范式（MP-Former），系统探索如何让机器人在异构平台与多变场景下实现高质量运动规划。
</div>

<div class="lang-en" markdown="1">
Industrial robots must operate in cluttered, dynamic workspaces, where classical planning methods struggle with scalability and adaptability. I explore adaptive sampling (multi-tree RRT), neural architectures (BRMPNet bidirectional recurrent networks), and sequence-to-sequence generation (MP-Former) to enable high-quality motion planning across heterogeneous robotic platforms and environments.
</div>

### <span class="lang-zh">2. 多机器人系统如何在无中心控制的情况下高效协调？</span><span class="lang-en">2. How Can Multiple Robots Coordinate Efficiently Without Centralized Control?</span>

<div class="lang-zh" markdown="1">
随着自主移动机器人在智能工厂中大规模部署，集中式协调成为可扩展性瓶颈。我研究基于**模仿学习**与**选择性通信**的去中心化多智能体路径规划（MAPF），同时探索前序约束任务分配与路径规划的一体化框架，使机器人群体无需全局调度即可高效协同——为柔性制造与仓储自动化提供关键支撑。
</div>

<div class="lang-en" markdown="1">
As autonomous mobile robots proliferate in smart factories, centralized coordination becomes a scalability bottleneck. I investigate decentralized multi-agent path planning (MAPF) through **imitation learning** and **selective communication**, alongside integrated frameworks for precedence-constrained task assignment and path finding — enabling robot fleets to coordinate efficiently without global control overhead, a key enabler for flexible manufacturing and warehouse automation.
</div>

### <span class="lang-zh">3. 机器人如何与人类安全、自然地协作？</span><span class="lang-en">3. How Can Robots Collaborate Safely and Naturally with Humans?</span>

<div class="lang-zh" markdown="1">
人机协作要求机器人同时解读人类意图、适应人类行为并保障操作安全——这是一个本质上相互耦合的难题。我构建了融合**人体数字孪生**与**混合现实**的运动规划协同进化框架，开发了基于**持续知识图谱嵌入**的自适应操作系统，并探索面向可变形物体协作搬运的**多模态强化学习**方法，从多个维度推进人机协作的安全性与自然性。
</div>

<div class="lang-en" markdown="1">
Human-robot collaboration requires robots to simultaneously interpret human intent, adapt to human behavior, and ensure safety — an inherently coupled challenge. I develop co-evolution frameworks combining **human digital twin** models and **mixed reality** for safe motion planning, continual **knowledge graph embedding** for adaptive manipulation, and **multi-modal reinforcement learning** for collaborative transport of deformable objects — advancing human-robot collaboration from multiple dimensions.
</div>

### <span class="lang-zh">4. 如何通过知识驱动方法赋予机器人跨任务泛化能力？</span><span class="lang-en">4. How Can Knowledge-Driven AI Enable Generalizable Robot Intelligence Across Tasks?</span>

<div class="lang-zh" markdown="1">
工业机器人频繁面对新工具、新工件和新任务，依赖手工编程的传统方式难以快速部署。我研究如何将**结构化知识**（图嵌入、可供性表示、数字孪生）与**数据驱动优化**（贝叶斯优化、强化学习、视觉语言模型）融合，使机器人具备跨任务泛化能力，降低部署门槛，加速智能制造落地。
</div>

<div class="lang-en" markdown="1">
Industrial robots frequently encounter new tools, workpieces, and tasks, making traditional manual programming impractical for rapid deployment. I investigate how **structured knowledge** (graph embeddings, affordance representations, digital twins) can be combined with **data-driven optimization** (Bayesian optimization, reinforcement learning, vision-language models) to enable robots to generalize across tasks — reducing deployment effort and accelerating the realization of intelligent manufacturing.
</div>
