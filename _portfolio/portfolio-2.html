On one hand, due to the buffer role of surface waves, air-side momentum flux is not identical to the ocean-side momentum flux. Under extreme wind conditions, the air-sea interface is no longer a distinct two-layer structure. Instead, a vapor-liquid mixed layer is formed with the air filling with sea spray droplets and the water filling with bubbles. Within this layer, the wind stress tends to saturate with the increase of wind speed. On the other hand, breaking waves produces large amounts of sea spray droplets.
